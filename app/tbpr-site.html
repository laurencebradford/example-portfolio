The task

TBPR is a wonderful, small-sized firm that is great at what they do. However, their website needed a makeover. Not only was the site not responsive, it also lacked Schema, Open Graph Protocol, and other markup key to SEO.

The site needed a major overhaul. The only thing that would stay intact would be the site's architecture and most of the content (about page, contact page, etc.).

What I did

In order to refresh the site, I took on a 6 step approach.

  1. Current site analysis - I took an indepth look at the front-end and backend. I also looked at the current design plus content.
  2. Ideation - After digging around the existing site, I gave suggestions on how the new site should appear.
  3. Mockups - Next, I created loose wireframes of what I envisioned the new site would look like. After a few revisions, ready to go.
  4. Development - After getting approval on the new site's look and feel, I got to coding. I built a fresh WordPress theme using the Bootstrap framework. As I developed, I made sure to add necessary Schema and microformat markup. The longest phase by far, but by the end had the new site theme live.
  5. SEO optimization - Aftwarwards, I went back in and added appropriate meta titles and descriptions using the SEO Yoast plugin, one of my favorites
  6. Final touches - Aside from redoing the site's theme, I also had the TBPR site move over to a better hosting plan, which sped up the site tremendously. I also set up static assets to be hosted on a CDN, which also helped tremendously with speed.

Outcome

The new TBPR site is not only mobile-friendly, but also developmed in a way that is better for ranking in the search engines and intergrates with social networks.

Specifically, Tina Brown has reported that:

  1. Average time on page increased by one minute
  2. The domain authority ranking improved by 7 points
  3. Overall, the number of new leads has shot up—proving the importance of a modern site
